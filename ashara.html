Majlis 3 / 1448H

Architecture & Engineering

4th al-Muharram al-Haraam

Dedicated to Nabi Ibrahim AS, builder of the Kaʿba, this majlis used the seven defining features of a masjid — as described by Syedna Jaʿfar bin Mansoor al-Yemen RA: land, four walls, roof, and air — as a framework for architecture, life-planning, and child-rearing.

Zameen (site selection): the choice of sacred sites — Bayt Allah, al-Mahdiyya, al-Mansuriyya, Darse Saifee, Raudat Tahera — as a model for choosing environments for prayer, rest, and a child's upbringing.

The walls: foundation through taqwa and tawakkul (Ibrahim's AS placement of the Hajar-e-Aswad), encompassment and utility (homes as "palaces," balancing deen and dunya), height and ambition (the Kaʿba's humble form versus its towering spiritual pull), and groundedness — balancing lofty aspiration with feasibility, illustrated by the flaws of Fallingwater house and the wisdom of Rasul Allah's SAW amnesty terms at the conquest of Makkah.

Roof and ventilation: lofty intention (niyyat), illustrated by Syedi Abdulqadir Hakimuddin's QR rejection of worldly reward for spiritual duty, and flexibility — always having a "plan B" in raising children.

The majlis closed with Imam Husain's AS unshakeable tawakkul in Karbala and his final, moving exchange with Maulatona Zainab AS.
